Habitat

Lions primarily inhabit savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands across sub-Saharan Africa. They require large territories that provide ample prey and cover for hunting.

Diet

Lions are carnivorous and primarily hunt large herbivores such as zebras, wildebeests, and antelopes. They are also known to scavenge and will eat smaller animals when larger prey is scarce.

Behavior

Lions live in social groups called prides, consisting of related females, their offspring, and a small number of adult males. They are known for their cooperative hunting strategies and territorial behavior.

Conservation Status

Lions are currently listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss, human-wildlife conflicts, and poaching. Conservation efforts focus on habitat protection, anti-poaching measures, and reducing human-lion conflicts.
