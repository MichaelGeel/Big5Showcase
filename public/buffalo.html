Habitat

Cape Buffaloes are found in a variety of habitats, including savannas, swamps and forests. They require areas with abundant water and grasslands for grazing.

Diet

Buffaloes are herbivores, primarily grazing on grasses but also consuming herbs and shrubs. They spend a significant portion of their day feeding and require large quantities of water

Behavior

Cape Buffaloes live in large, cohesive herds for protection against predators. They are known for their strong herd instincts and will fiercely defend members of their group.

Conservation Status

Cape buffaloes are listed as least concern, but they face threats from habitat loss and diseases such as bovine tubercolusis. Conservation efforts focus on habitat preservation and disease management.
