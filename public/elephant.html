Habitat

African elephants are found in a variety of habitats, including savannas, forests, deserts and marshes. They require large areas of land to support their needs for food, water and social interaction.

Diet

Elephants are herbivores and consume a diverse diet of grasses, fruits, bark and leaves. They can eat up to 300 points of vegetation in a single day.

Behavior

Elephants are highly social animals, living in matriarchal herds led by an older female. They are known for their intelligence, strong family bonds, and complex communication methods, including vocalizations and body languages.

Conservation Status

Elephants are listed as vulnerable due to threats from poaching for ivory and habitat loss. Conservation initiates include anti-poaching patrols, habitat restoration, and legal protection of elephant populations.
