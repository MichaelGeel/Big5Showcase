Habitat

Leopards are highly adaptable and can be found in a range of habitats, from forests and mountains to savannas and grasslands. They prefer areas with dense vegetation for cover and are also found in urban areas.

Diet

Leopards are carnivorous and have a varied diet including antelope, deer, rodents and birds. They are opportunistic hunters and are known for their ability to carry prey up trees to avoid scavengers.

Behavior

Leopards are solitary and nocturnal animals, known for their stealth and strength. They are excellent climbers and swimmers, often using these skills to hunt and evade threats.

Conservation Status

Leopards are listed as vulnerable, with threats including habitat fragmentation, human-wildlife conflict, and illegal hunting. Conservation efforts focus on habitat preservation and reducing human-leopard conflicts.
